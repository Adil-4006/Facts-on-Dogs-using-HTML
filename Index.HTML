Facts about Dogs

Variety of Breeds: There are over 340 dog breeds recognized worldwide, each with unique traits and characteristics.
Sense of Smell: Dogs have an incredible sense of smell, with up to 300 million olfactory receptors in their noses, compared to about 6 million in humans. This makes them excellent at detecting scents.
Communication: Dogs communicate using a combination of vocalizations (like barking), body language, and facial expressions. They can understand human emotions and respond accordingly.
They do Dogs are known for their loyalty and often form strong bonds with their human companions. They can be incredibly protective of their families.
Intelligence: Many dog breeds are highly intelligent and capable of learning commands, tricks, and tasks. Some breeds, like Border Collies, are known for their problem-solving abilities.
Lifespan: The average lifespan of a dog varies by breed, but most dogs live between 10 to 15 years. Smaller breeds often live longer than larger breeds.
Social Animals: Dogs are pack animals by nature, meaning they thrive in social settings. They often enjoy being part of a family and can experience separation anxiety if left alone for too long.
Herding and Working: Many breeds have been developed for specific tasks, such as herding, hunting, or guarding. This has shaped their behavior and instincts.

The dog is a domesticated descendant of the wolf. Also called the domestic dog, it is derived from extinct Pleistocene wolves, and the modern wolf is the dog's nearest living relative. Dogs were the first species to be domesticated by hunter-gatherers over 15,000 years ago before the development of agriculture.
